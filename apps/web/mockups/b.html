Fact Layer

  • F-01[sample] Site latitude ~89°S: near-continuous solar exposure on ridge crests, with permanently shadowed regions within rover range.
  • F-02[sample] Regolith overburden of ~2.5 m over habitat modules targets GCR dose reduction for long-stay crews.
  • F-03[sample] Lunar night at lower latitudes lasts ~14 Earth days; the polar site trades this for grazing light and long shadows.
  • F-04[sample] Surface gravity 1.62 m/s² drives structure sizing, dust dynamics, and crew locomotion in the scene.
  • F-05[sample] One-way light time to Earth ~1.3 s; comms relay shown in orbit covers far-side and PSR operations.
